ABOUT

The idea of .flare came to life in Alberta, Canada by the owners of a small local and family-owned furniture shop. The owners, a father and his daughters are established lovers of nature and early on in their business they became obsessed with the beautiful wood found in Alberta: maple, oak and birch. With the hard work and the refined taste of the owners, .flar has today established its name in the industry.

Offering beautiful, custom-made furniture of highest quality, .flare furniture is carefully crafted by the hands of the very owners and their business partners in the very workshop where it all started. Timeless, minimalistic creations of highest quality woods and eco-friendly cotton will not only provide your home with beautifully designed pieces, but also offer you the comfort you deserve and long for.

.flare offers a sortiment of custom-made furniture pieces such as chairs, sofas, shelves and desks. We take your ideas into consideration, marry them with our timeless design and consult with you every step of the process.

materials and fabrics

Maple

Maple trees have been the symbol of the country of Canada since the 1700's. We source this beautiful, historic and durable wood from Eastern part of Canada. Our maple trees are recycled and obtained with the most eco-friendly methods, which perserve the sweet smell of the wood.

Oak

This majestic, tall and sturdy wood has been growing on the teritory of Ontario for centuries. As this beutiful trees comes in veriety of species, we source our oak in the provinces of Ontatio, British Columbia and even Manitoba. Traces of natural shading might be present.

Birch

This charming, irregularly-rounded tree grows with dark green leaves both in the province of Quebec as well as Saskatchewan. Canadian natives used birches for canoes, baskets and kitchen utensils. Today the wood is widely used for furniture and veneers.

Organic Cotton

The organic cotton used for the production of our fabrics covering sofas is sourced locally, from a mid-size family-owned farm just outside of Ontario. Due to its eco-friendly processing methods, the material has a potential of feeling slightly rougher under a touch.
